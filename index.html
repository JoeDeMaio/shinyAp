Ellipse Problem

The parimeter of an ellipse can not be determined exactly, it must be approximated. The purpose of this ap is to calculate the lenght of an arch formed from half an ellipse

These sliders calclate the length of the arch. Move the sliders to input the horizontal and axes of the ellipse.

Reference: Math is Fun, Perimeter of an ellipse: https://www.mathsisfun.com/geometry/ellipse-perimeter.html
I used Ramanjan's first approximation: \[ p \approx \pi \biggl[3(a+b)- \sqrt{(3a+b)(a+3b)} \biggl] \]

The lenght of the arch is half of the perimeter of the ellipse (p/2).
